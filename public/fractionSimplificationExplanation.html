Simplifying a fraction means writing it using the smallest numbers but keeping the same amount. We do this by dividing the top and bottom by the same number (called a common factor).
\(\tfrac{4}{8}\)

\(\tfrac{1}{2}\)
Why does \(\tfrac{4}{8}\) become \(\tfrac{1}{2}\)? Because both 4 and 8 can be divided by 4.
\(4 \\div 4 = 1\) and \(8 \\div 4 = 2\)
